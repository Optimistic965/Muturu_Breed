OUR STORY

Cattle are the most important livestock species that supply meat with contributions from poultry, sheep and goats and other forms of livestock including bush meat.

The indigenous Muturu cattle (MC) found almost in all the geopolitical zones in Nigeria have maintained a sub-optimal level of performance compared to the White Fulani (WF) cattle breed which is the most predominant in Nigeria, making up to 51% of the national cattle herd.
About 99% of the MC population managed under the traditional pastoral system rely predominantly on natural range resources and crop residues.

CHALLENGES

  • Lockdown as a result of COVID-19 pandemic had great effects on the execution of the research.
  • Land preparation was delayed and prolonged due to scarcity of equipment such as bulldozer and Rome plough needed for different operations.
  • Astronomical increase in the prices of goods imposed a setback on the availability and purchase of materials needed for implementation of the research.
  • Unfavourable weather conditions. There was an absolute change in the rainfall pattern in the southwest, especially in Abeokuta which adversely affected land preparation and pasture establishment.
  • Scarcity of experimental animals (Muturu Cattle).

IMPACTS

  • Establishment of almost 4ha of pasture land planted with elephant and guinea grasses, which is novel within the university campus.
  • Enlightenment of Muturu cattle rearers on improved breeding and good management of the animals.
  • Semen collection and evaluation.
  • Scanning and Artificial Insemination of the Muturu cows.
  • Intensive management of Muturu cattle.
  • Silage making: The cattle were fed silage made from Guinea and Elephant grasses during the dry season.

MISSION AND VISION

    Mission

  • Improve the phenotypic and genetic characteristics of the Muturu cattle for rapid multiplication in Nigeria.
  • Determine the productive enhancement of proven Muturu cows and bulls mated through Artificial insemination.
  • Determine the performance of intensively managed Muturu calves fed different sources of supplementary diets.
  • Build capacity of stakeholders in Muturu cattle beef value chain capabilities.

    Vision

  • To bridge the gap in the beef value chain in Nigeria by improving the performance of the breeding stock of indigenous Muturu cattle under improved feeding and management.
